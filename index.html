Featured Project:
NY County Mental Health Outreach Analysis

An exploratory data analysis project examining county-level community outreach expenditure for mental health services in New York. This project explores patterns across counties and highlights differences in public spending through visual analysis. For example, as we can see visually, spending peaked around 2009–2010 before steadily declining through 2016. This indicates a potential shift in funding priorities over time.

Interactive Mapping
& Geographic Comparison Analysis

This work includes map-based visualization to compare expenditure across counties, making geographic patterns easier to interpret and communicate. The data shows clear regional disparities in mental health outreach funding across New York counties, with some areas consistently receiving higher investment than others. These differences suggest underlying variations in population needs, service utilization, or policy priorities. Understanding these gaps is key to improving equitable distribution of mental health resources statewide.
